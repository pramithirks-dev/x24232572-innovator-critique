The Problem

A 5,000-year-old conflict meets a one-hectare farm.

Across the Western Ghats, smallholder coconut farmers and Asian elephants are colliding on the same shrinking land. Existing solutions — electric fences, firecrackers, trenches — wound the animals or traumatise the herds. Compensation arrives 14 months too late.

  • 01
    Lethal deterrents fail everyone

    Electric fences electrocute calves; firecrackers trigger stampedes that injure both species.

  • 02
    Compensation is broken

    State payouts arrive 12–18 months after damage and cover ~15% of actual loss.

  • 03
    Smallholders bear the cost

    180,000 households earn under ₹1.2 lakh/year and lose up to a quarter of it to nightly raids.

  • 04
    Elephants are sacred — and disappearing

    India has lost 60% of its wild elephant corridors in 30 years. The species cannot afford another adversary.

The Solution

One pod. One drone.
Zero cracker fire.

The GajaLens Sentinel Pod is a solar-powered perimeter station with a tethered AI drone — Karuna — that sees in the dark, recognises individual elephants, and deploys species-appropriate deterrents your grandmother would approve of.

How it works

From detection to deterrent in four seconds.

  • i.
    Dusk patrol

    Karuna auto-launches at sunset and sweeps the perimeter on a tethered 200-metre leash.

  • ii.
    Edge detection

    Onboard YOLOv9 flags an elephant, classifies herd size, identifies individuals.

  • iii.
    Graduated response

    Infrasound rumble first. Bee-buzz next. Forest-officer ping if needed. Never lethal.

  • iv.
    Dawn delivery

    You wake to a WhatsApp message — and a portrait of last night's visitor walking home.
