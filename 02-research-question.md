# Research Question

**How can autonomous AI-vision drones, deployed at the perimeter of smallholder coconut plantations bordering elephant migration corridors in South India, reduce human–elephant conflict (HEC) incidents and crop loss without compromising elephant welfare or movement, compared with conventional deterrents (electric fencing, crackers, trenches)?**

## Sub-questions
1. What detection accuracy and response latency must an edge-AI drone achieve to deter an approaching herd before the first crop contact event?
2. Which non-lethal acoustic and visual cues (e.g., low-frequency rumbles, bee-buzz playback, pulsed light) are statistically most effective at redirecting wild Asian elephants (*Elephas maximus*) without causing chronic stress?
3. Can individual re-identification of elephants via computer vision (ear notches, tusk shape, scar patterns) enable adaptive deterrent strategies that decrease habituation over a 12-month period?
4. What is the unit economics tipping point at which an AI-drone-as-a-service subscription becomes cheaper than annual crop loss + state compensation claims for a sub-2-hectare smallholder?
