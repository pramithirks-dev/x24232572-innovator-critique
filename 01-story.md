# The Coconut Watcher

In the moonlit coconut groves of Kerala, an old farmer named Ravi noticed his crop vanishing night after night to a herd of elephants drifting in from the Western Ghats. One dawn, his granddaughter Meera arrived from Bengaluru with a small AI-powered drone that hummed softly above the palms, its lens catching the silver gleam of tusks moving between the trees. Instead of firing crackers or stringing electric fences, Meera taught the drone to recognise each elephant by the curl of its ears and the pattern of its scars, and to send a polite, low-frequency rumble that nudged the herd back toward the forest before sunrise. Within a season, Ravi's harvest doubled, the elephants kept their ancient corridor, and the photographs the drone captured each night — of matriarchs leading calves through dew-soaked fronds — were exhibited in Kochi as the world's first wildlife portrait series shot entirely by an algorithm with kindness in its code.
